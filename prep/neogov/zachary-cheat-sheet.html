10-Minute Zachary Schmidt Cram Cheat Sheet

The most load-bearing things to absorb before tomorrow's call. Read this 30 minutes before you walk in.

1. The lens reframe

You handled Nick's "will Thom move our revenue motion" lens well. Zachary's lens is different:

"Will this senior architect fit cleanly into our application stack and respect our IT processes, or will he be a cowboy?"

He's not asking can you build the AI. He's asking will you operate it inside my application portfolio without breaking my Salesforce admin's day.

2. Who Zachary actually is

He IS Director of Enterprise Applications (per Nick, who championed you forward). LinkedIn still shows his prior "Director of Help Desk" title — that's just a stale profile, which is actually a positive signal (he's thriving in the new role, not updating personal brand).

The composite — Director of Enterprise Applications who came up through Help Desk — is a more substantial profile than either label alone:

  • Owns the full Enterprise Applications scope: Salesforce platform strategy, NetSuite, RightRev, integration plane, Okta SSO/IAM, application portfolio, vendor management, SOC 2 posture, AI substrate strategy
  • Has the help-desk-up career heritage giving him deep ops chops, identity mastery, ITIL discipline, automation-builder instincts
  • Career arc: Hulu → DNAM Apparel → MasterClass → NEOGOV (~2.5 years, promoted to current scope along the way)
  • B.A. Communication (not CS, not MBA). Self-taught into IT leadership.
  • Okta Certified Administrator + Professional; ITIL 4 Foundation; Python automation
  • Vocabulary: tickets reduced, applications consolidated, dollars saved, hours automated away — even in the bigger role

Critical implication: he's making strategic architectural decisions about NEOGOV's application stack RIGHT NOW (including the AI substrate strategy for the Revenue AI work). He's not just evaluating whether you'll respect his processes — he's evaluating whether you can be a peer-partner on the architectural decisions he's currently owning.

3. The strongest single sentence to have ready

If he opens with "how would you work with internal IT" or "what's your operating model" (very likely):

"I've spent forty years building production AI inside complicated application stacks — semiconductor lines, freight brokerage, Microsoft's seller platform. The pattern that holds: the AI system has to live inside the existing application portfolio, not next to it. My first weeks would be learning your Salesforce footprint, your Okta tenancy, your ticketing and change-control patterns. The AI architecture comes second; the integration architecture and operating model come first."

Memorize the SHAPE, not the words.

4. NEOGOV's confirmed stack (so you sound informed)

  • Salesforce (Service Cloud, CPQ, Revenue Cloud, Billing, Communities, CRM Analytics)
  • NetSuite (financial ERP)
  • RightRev (revenue automation, ASC 606)
  • Okta (SSO / IAM — Zachary's lane)
  • Google Workspace (incl. Google Chat — Zachary migrated them off Slack)
  • Outreach, Gong, Tableau, 6Sense, Pursuit.us, Starbridge
  • Jira

Likely but not confirmed: FreshService for ITSM (his prior pattern), Workday for HRIS, Marketo for marketing automation, Snowflake for data warehouse.

5. His most likely questions

Strategic (may come early — he's actually making these decisions):

  1. "Build vs. buy — custom on OpenAI / Anthropic, Salesforce Agentforce, Snowflake Cortex?" → Probably hybrid pragmatic. "I'd expect to be a working partner with you on the substrate selection, not the sole architect of it. Depends on your Salesforce footprint, three-year roadmap, and risk posture on platform lock-in. Want to learn those constraints from you before I commit."
  2. "Walk me through connecting the AI workspace to our Salesforce data." → Service accounts with scoped read access on objects you actually need; warehouse layer between Salesforce and AI (governor limits, cleaner auth); narrow writes back as custom objects with provenance, never overwriting Stage / Amount.

Operational (his help-desk-up heritage means these carry intense weight):

  1. "How would you handle service accounts and Okta-enforced access?" → Per integration, not per app; minimum-scope read; auth through Okta where supported; secrets in managed vault; naming convention obvious in audit.
  2. "Who carries the pager when this breaks at 6 AM Monday?" → You for the first 90 days. Then shared on-call with the natural-home team, with documented runbooks for top 5 failure modes.
  3. "How do you handle change control for prompt and model changes?" → Prompts in version control, peer review, automated test set in CI, shadow → small % → full traffic, one-click rollback, change notifications go through THEIR channel (FreshService / ServiceNow / whatever).
  4. "What's the audit trail?" → Every artifact has unique ID → input snapshot + prompt versions + model version + tool calls + intermediate results. Govtech audit trails matter.

Partnership / tone test:

  1. "How have you worked with internal IT in past roles?" → Concrete story. Treat application admin team as system experts; bring AI architectural judgment; intersection is where good decisions land.

6. What to avoid

  • Don't lead with AI architecture. Lead with integration and operating model. Application stack is the outer shell; AI is the inner layer.
  • Don't critique the existing stack. Even if you have opinions.
  • Don't claim deep Okta / NetSuite expertise you don't have. "Familiar paradigm, would ramp on your specific tenancy" is the right answer.
  • Don't pile credentials. He has your resume. Help-desk-up directors are wary of senior-architect resumes.
  • Don't surface Nick's "overqualified" concern. Different worry; don't pre-defend.
  • Don't ask about salary, remote, or side consulting. Wrong round.
  • Don't volunteer opinions on rebuilding things. Lead with "I'd want to understand the existing stack first."

7. Questions to ask him (have 2-3 ready)

  1. "What does the integration map look like today between Salesforce, NetSuite, and the rest of the stack? Where are the brittle seams?"
  2. "What's your ITSM tool of choice at NEOGOV — FreshService, ServiceNow, something else?"
  3. "How do IT and RevOps work together when there's a new system to deploy?"
  4. ★ "What would success look like at six months for this role from your seat — not from Nick's?" (The strongest one — invites him to articulate his lens.)

Closing posture

Three things to demonstrate in 30 minutes:

  1. You think in integration architecture, not just AI architecture
  2. You respect IT processes (change control, ticketing, audit trails, service-account hygiene)
  3. You're a partner to his team, not a competitor for it

Walk in calm. Speak in his idiom. Let him stay the senior in the application-stack conversation.

You've got this.
