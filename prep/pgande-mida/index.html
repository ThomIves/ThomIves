PG&E MIDA · Computer Vision

Last updated 2026-06-26

Comprehensive prep for PG&E's MIDA program (Multi-modal Inspection Data Annotation, built on Palantir Foundry). MIDA uses Vision Language Models (currently Gemini 3.1) to find electric assets — poles, transformers, splices, circuits, attachments — in drone, helicopter, and satellite imagery, and to detect unauthorized attachments and damage patterns.

MIDA is part of (or adjacent to) the larger EPIC 4.22 program — a CPUC-funded multi-vendor Computer Vision demonstration for electric asset management.
