10-Minute Charles Scoma Cram Cheat Sheet

Read this at 4:55 PM. Walk in at 5:05 PM.

1. Who Charles is (memorize this)

  • AI Lead at PG&E for high-visibility projects including MIDA
  • PG&E veteran: 6+ years employee → degree → came back as contractor
  • PG&E "blindly trusts him" (Awais's words). His vote = the decision.
  • Will probe technical depth. "Charles will be in more technical."
  • He came back voluntarily. Mission belief is real for him.

He's senior, measured, decisive. Don't ramble. Match his cadence.

2. What this 15-min screen is testing

  1. Senior CV fluency (3-5 min of probing)
  2. Production AI maturity (deployment + evaluation + change-control thinking)
  3. Judgment in high-stakes context (asks questions before proposing)
  4. Could he work with you (tone, posture, listening signal)

NOT testing: salary, logistics, full career history.

3. The opening "tell me about yourself" — ~50 seconds

"Computer Vision Engineer and AI Applications Architect — 40 years. M.S. at Texas A&M on Robot Calibration and Computer Image Controlled Robotics; three IEEE Transactions on Robotics & Automation publications followed. Built production CV at ON Semi on a semiconductor manufacturing line — image defect detection on imaging die using iterative robust regression and spatial clustering on residual anomaly maps. Right now at Microsoft via Unify Consulting on multimodal foundation model production AI. The thread that ties it together is production CV where the cost of being wrong is high. That's what drew me to PG&E MIDA."

4. The "why PG&E" — ~30 seconds

"PG&E is mission-critical CV work where every detection or miss has downstream consequences — grid reliability, public safety, wildfire mitigation. That's the kind of CV work I want to be doing. The MIDA program specifically is at the edge of what multimodal foundation models can do — VLLM-based auto-labeling at scale — which is exactly where I'm operating now with Microsoft. It maps."

5. The ON Semi Streak Test story — ~90 seconds (use if he asks about a CV project)

Parametric illumination model (5th-degree bivariate poly to background dome) → iterative robust regression with outlier suppression → spatial clustering / connected-component analysis on residual anomaly map → 3-week PITAD production release cycle. Removed 2+ man-weeks manual tuning per product; matched experienced human inspectors without fatigue; stopped being a top-three RMA-driver. Engineering constraint: Python 2.5 + numpy only — wrote linear algebra and stats from scratch. (That constraint detail lands.)

6. Quick-fire technical answers

CV evaluation metrics: Precision, Recall, F1, per-class recall at fixed precision. Object detection: mAP at multiple IoU thresholds. For PG&E-scale: weight toward recall, watch precision as budget. Held-out test set mirroring production conditions (weather, season, time of day).

Validation split design: Split on time first (no leakage across acquisition periods), then on physical asset and camera. Synthetic data for training class balance; validation set stays real.

VLLMs vs fine-tuned: Cold-start / sparse-label → VLLMs. Lots-of-labels / cost-per-inference / low-latency → fine-tuned. Real answer is hybrid: VLLM auto-labels to bootstrap, fine-tune smaller specialized model for production inference, VLLM stays as human-review escalation tier.

"How would you approach MIDA?" — DO NOT propose immediately. Ask:

  1. What does success look like at program level — labels or operational outcomes?
  2. Data volume and cadence — real-time, batch, both?
  3. Current state — augmenting an existing pipeline or greenfield?
  4. Consequence shape — what kind of misses are most expensive?

THEN propose: foundation-model selection, structured-output design, evaluation scaffolding, human-in-the-loop, production observability.

7. Questions to ask Charles (have 2 ready)

★ The strongest: "You came back to PG&E as a contractor after time away — what made you want to come back?" (Honors his journey. Surfaces his mission lens.)

★ The closer: "If I joined, what would success at 6 months look like from your seat — not from HR's?"

Backup: "What's the current state of the MIDA program — v0 pipeline or earlier?" or "What VLLMs is the team currently using or evaluating?"

8. What to avoid

  • Don't lead with multi-physics PhD or 40-years framing
  • Don't lead with operator credentials (real estate, financial services)
  • Don't pretend to know PG&E-internal tools or pipelines
  • Don't pile credentials — he has your resume
  • Don't volunteer opinions on Waldo, EPIC programs, or PG&E politics
  • Don't ramble — crisp answers, then stop
  • Don't bring up salary, location, 1099, or Integrating Consulting
  • Don't telegraph the Unify timeline
  • Don't ask to record

Walk in calm. Camera + audio tested 5 min before. Glass of water nearby.

Three things to demonstrate:

  1. Senior production CV fluency (ON Semi detail + VLLM/foundation-model thinking)
  2. Mission-critical posture (consequence-shape framing)
  3. Partner mindset (questions before proposing, peer respect)

Match his cadence. Let him drive. End by asking him the strongest question.
