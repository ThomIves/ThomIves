2. The format (per Awais, updated 2026-06-30)

  • Charles spends ~2 min briefing you on the work and how it relates to your experience
  • You then drive ~13 min of conversation through your questions
  • Be technical — CNNs, YOLO, foundation models, whatever maps to your real work

Your questions are the primary signal. Not your answers.

What he's testing:

  1. Quality of your questions (senior systems thinking or surface stuff?)

  3. Technical depth in context (no resume-reciting)
  4. Senior posture (measured, peer-level)

NOT testing: salary, logistics, full career history.

5. The ON Semi Streak Test story — ~90 seconds (use if he asks about a CV project)

Parametric illumination model (5th-degree bivariate poly to background dome) → iterative robust regression with outlier suppression → spatial clustering / connected-component analysis on residual anomaly map → 3-week PITAD production release cycle. Removed 2+ man-weeks manual tuning per product; matched experienced human inspectors without fatigue; stopped being a top-three RMA-driver. Engineering constraint: Python 2.5 + numpy only — wrote linear algebra and stats from scratch. (That constraint detail lands.)

6. Quick-fire technical answers

CV evaluation metrics: Precision, Recall, F1, per-class recall at fixed precision. Object detection: mAP at multiple IoU thresholds. For PG&E-scale: weight toward recall, watch precision as budget. Held-out test set mirroring production conditions (weather, season, time of day).

Validation split design: Split on time first (no leakage across acquisition periods), then on physical asset and camera. Synthetic data for training class balance; validation set stays real.

VLLMs vs fine-tuned: Cold-start / sparse-label → VLLMs. Lots-of-labels / cost-per-inference / low-latency → fine-tuned. Real answer is hybrid: VLLM auto-labels to bootstrap, fine-tune smaller specialized model for production inference, VLLM stays as human-review escalation tier.

"How would you approach MIDA?" — DO NOT propose immediately. Ask:

  1. What does success look like at program level — labels or operational outcomes?
  2. Data volume and cadence — real-time, batch, both?
  3. Current state — augmenting an existing pipeline or greenfield?
  4. Consequence shape — what kind of misses are most expensive?

THEN propose: foundation-model selection, structured-output design, evaluation scaffolding, human-in-the-loop, production observability.

6. ★ CLOSER (last 2-3 min): "If I joined, what would success at 6 months look like from your seat — not from HR's?"

Bonus human question if natural space: "You came back to PG&E as a contractor after time away — what made you want to come back?"
