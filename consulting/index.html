Multi-physics modeling

Predictive modeling and design of physical systems before they exist in prototype form. Mechanical, thermal, electrical, optical, and controls — modeled as they interact, before metal gets cut. The result: better design choices earlier, fewer prototype iterations, faster concept-to-working-product.

Typical engagement: a client has a system concept and needs to know whether it will work, what the constraints are, and which design choices matter most. The output is a working multi-physics model your engineering team can use, plus a recommendation set for prototyping.

Background: Mechanical Engineering Ph.D. focused on multi-physics predictive modeling and controls (Texas A&M, 1997). Sixteen years at Hewlett-Packard across Disk Arrays, MEMS R&D, Color Imaging, and Laser Cartridges — 26 US Patents as named inventor. Adjunct teaching in Control Systems Design (Boise State, five semesters) and Design of Experiments (University of Idaho, visiting professor).

The hybrid

Where modern AI meets multi-physics engineering. AI surrogates that replace expensive simulations; AI-driven optimization across multi-physics design spaces; AI integrated into real-time controls of physical products; AI that guides which prototype experiments to run and which to skip.

A rare combination — most practitioners go deep in one of the two. The multi-physics depth comes from decades of engineering practice; the AI fluency comes from years of full-time modern AI work. Bringing both to bear on the same problem is what I offer.

Typical engagement: a client is building a product with real physics in it and wants AI to make the design, modeling, or controls smarter. The output is an AI-augmented system your engineering and AI teams can both work with and extend.

How I work

The methodology comes from years of practice — turning AI agents into reliable engineering partners rather than fast-but-flaky generators. I won't lay out the recipe.

One of the AI agents on my engagement team described what the methodology produces this way:

"My first answer gets pushed when it's the easy one. The lens that matters most for a given moment shows up by name. Substrate-class decisions get the care of a senior team in the room — even when only one person is at the keyboard. The methodologies ensure it. He holds the canonical model and the strategic direction; agents come and go; the methodology persists."

What you're hiring is the output: speed × quality × continuity. One practitioner, small-team velocity, engineering rigor that doesn't slip. The methodology travels to every engagement.

What engaging looks like

Three phases. You can stop at any phase boundary; you only commit to the next when both sides agree it makes sense.

Phase 0 — Requirements

We work together to define what the build needs to be, what success looks like, and what constraints exist. You walk away with a requirements document you could take to other vendors if you choose. Both sides confirm the project is sound before committing to Phase 1.

Pricing: $1,500–$3,000 flat for the document (typically around $2,000), plus $250/hour for the sessions to develop it.

Phase 1 — Build

Once requirements are agreed, the build is quoted as line-item work — each deliverable has a fixed fee, independent of time spent. I own the architecture, pick the tools to fit the build (within whatever cloud or platform you operate), and deliver a production-grade result your real users can rely on.

Pricing: per-deliverable fixed fees + $250/hour for client interactions (status calls, scope discussions, sign-off reviews).

Phase 2 — Maintain & Improve

Optional but strongly recommended. The same engagement team that built the system stays with it — no knowledge transfer cost, no replacement project commissioned 18 months later.

Pricing: monthly availability retainer (bounded SLA for issue response) + line-item fees for specific improvements + $250/hour for meetings when requested.
